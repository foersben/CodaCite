# Workflows

The lifecycle of a document within the Enterprise Omni-Copilot begins with a rigorous ingestion process designed to handle a multitude of unstructured formats. Upon entry, the raw text is subjected to an intelligent chunking strategy that breaks the document into semantically coherent segments, preserving the contextual integrity necessary for deep analysis. These segments then enter the graph extraction phase, where advanced models parse the text to identify key entities and the nuanced relationships connecting them. Simultaneously, a coreference resolution mechanism operates to ensure that disparate mentions of the same entity across the text are unified, solidifying the accuracy and cohesion of the resulting knowledge representation. This newly structured intelligence is then seamlessly integrated into the overarching graph database, ready for subsequent querying.

When a user initiates a query, the retrieval process activates a sophisticated synthesis of techniques to surface the most relevant and contextualized answers. The system first employs a vector search to locate the most semantically proximate nodes within the knowledge base, establishing a foundational set of relevant information. From these initial nodes, the system executes multi-hop traversals across the graph, exploring the interconnected relationships to gather a comprehensive context that single-point retrieval methods would miss. Finally, the gathered information is subjected to a reranking process, which meticulously evaluates and orders the synthesized context to ensure the highest fidelity and relevance before presenting the fully formed insight to the user.
